TITLE:
THE LIMITS OF CLARITY

ARCHIVE ENTRY:

Clarity improves perception.

It does not eliminate mystery.

The Archive recognizes that some aspects of reality remain larger than observation.

Clarity allows the observer to see more accurately.

It does not grant total understanding.

This distinction matters.

Many confuse clarity with mastery.

The Archive does not.

The clearer the observer becomes,

the more visible the horizon of the unknown becomes.

RECOVERED FRAGMENT:

Clarity reveals how much remains unrecovered.
