TITLE:
THE DANGER OF CERTAINTY

ARCHIVE ENTRY:

Ignorance is rarely the greatest obstacle to recovery.

Certainty often is.

The observer who believes they already understand ceases to observe.

Questions disappear.

Curiosity fades.

Recovery slows.

Humility protects observation.

It preserves openness.

It preserves adaptability.

It preserves learning.

The Archive therefore treats certainty with caution.

Recovery remains possible only while discovery remains possible.

RECOVERED FRAGMENT:

The end of questioning is often the end of recovery.
