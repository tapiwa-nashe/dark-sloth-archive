TITLE:
THE ARCHITECTURE OF NOISE

ARCHIVE ENTRY:

Noise does more than interrupt.

Noise shapes perception.

Noise redirects attention.

Noise determines what remains visible.

The Archive teaches that many people do not suffer from a lack of information.

They suffer from excess information.

Noise accumulates.

Meaning becomes difficult to distinguish.

The observer must therefore learn silence.

Not the absence of sound.

The recovery of space.

RECOVERED FRAGMENT:

Noise expands faster than meaning.
